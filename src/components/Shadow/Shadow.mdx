## Importance of Shadow
### 1. Depth and Dimension
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Shadows create the illusion of depth and dimensionality within the UI, making elements appear elevated or recessed relative to each other. By casting shadows, elements can visually pop out from the background, enhancing the overall perception of depth and realism.
</p>

<div></div>
### 2. Hierarchy and Emphasis
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Shadows help establish visual hierarchy by indicating the spatial relationship between different UI elements. For example, drop shadows behind elevated elements, such as buttons or cards, signify their prominence or importance within the UI hierarchy. Shadows can also be used to emphasize certain elements, such as highlighting active or interactive components.
</p>

### 3. Visual Separation
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Shadows provide visual separation between overlapping elements, preventing them from appearing flat or merged together. By casting shadows, elements are visually distinct from their surroundings, improving readability and clarity.
</p>

### 4. Contextual Awareness
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Shadows contribute to users' understanding of the UI's spatial layout and behavior. Dynamic shadows, such as those cast by floating elements or animated transitions, enhance users' perception of movement and interaction within the interface.
</p>
