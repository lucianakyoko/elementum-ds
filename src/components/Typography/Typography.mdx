## Use Cases
### 1. Branding
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Typography is an integral part of brand identity and recognition. Consistent use of fonts across digital platforms helps reinforce brand identity and create a cohesive brand experience. Fonts are selected based on their ability to convey the brand's personality and values, whether it's modern and sleek or traditional and elegant.
</p>

### 2. Visual Hierarchy
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Typography is used to establish visual hierarchy and guide users' attention to the most important elements on the screen. Different font sizes, weights, and styles are employed to differentiate between headings, body text, and other UI elements. Bold or larger fonts are used for headlines and primary calls to action, while lighter or smaller fonts are used for secondary information.
</p>

### 3. Emotional Response
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Typography can evoke emotional responses and convey the tone and mood of the UI. The choice of fonts, along with factors like spacing and alignment, can influence users' perceptions and behaviors. For example, serif fonts may convey a sense of tradition and authority, while sans-serif fonts may feel more modern and approachable.
</p>

### 4. Readability
<p style={{paddingBottom: '16px'}}>
  Typography directly impacts readability, which is crucial for user comprehension and engagement. Designers must select fonts that are easy to read on various screen sizes and devices. Factors like font size, line spacing, and contrast between text and background colors are carefully considered to ensure optimal readability and accessibility.
</p>
